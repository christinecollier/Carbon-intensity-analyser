What is Carbon Intensity?

The carbon intensity of electricity is a measure of how much CO2 emissions are produced per kilowatt hour of electricity consumed.

Live data and analysis

Access to real-time insights including: latest measured carbon intensity (CI), forecasted daily maximum and minimum CI, and a comparison of the measured and forecasted daily CI values.

Generation Mix

A break-down of the proportion of carbon intensity originating from a variety of fuel types including: gas, coal, nuclear, biomass, hydro, imports, solar, wind, and other.

48-hour forecast

NESO's Carbon Intensity API provides an indicative trend of regional carbon intensity of the electricity system in Great Britain up to 2 days ahead of real-time.
